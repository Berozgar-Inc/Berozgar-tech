Inside Infinity Con: India's Fandom Under One Roof

Cosplay, creator panels and controlled chaos — what it takes to bring every fandom together in one place.

A screening celebrates one title. A convention celebrates everything. Infinity Con is what happens when you stop asking fans to pick a lane — and build a room big enough for all of them at once.

Marvel diehards and DC loyalists. Potterheads and the Star Wars faithful. Anime, fantasy, sci-fi, the shows people quote like scripture. For one weekend, the rivalries are part of the fun and the only thing everyone agrees on is that this is their place.

More than a venue

Infinity Con is built like a world, not a hall. Cosplay walkways and photo moments. Creator panels and meet-and-greets. Games, merch drops, giveaways, and the kind of surprises that turn a queue into a story you tell later. Every corner is designed to be walked into, not just walked past.

Behind that ease is an enormous amount of operations — crowd flow, scheduling, ticketing, run-of-show — the invisible scaffolding that lets thousands of people feel like the day was made just for them.

The rivalries are part of the fun. The belonging is the whole point.

Why bring everyone together

Fandoms are usually scattered — a group chat here, a subreddit there, a screening once in a while. Infinity Con collapses that distance. Suddenly the person next to you gets the reference, finished the same series, has the same strong opinions. That recognition is rare, and it's the reason people travel for it.

Part of something bigger

Infinity Con sits inside everything Heroes United does — 390+ events, 56,000+ fans, 210+ partners across Mumbai, Bengaluru, Ahmedabad and Indore. The screenings build the community through the year; the con is where the whole community shows up at once.

What's next

Bigger floors, more creators, more cities — and more reasons to show up in costume. If you've ever felt like your fandom deserved a stage, Infinity Con is being built for exactly that.
