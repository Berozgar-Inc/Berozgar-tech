Turning Movie Tickets Into Cultural Moments

How Heroes United reinvented the fan screening — and why showtime is now an event worth remembering.

For decades, going to the movies meant the same thing everywhere: buy a ticket, find your seat, watch in the dark, leave. The film might be extraordinary, but the experience around it was identical — whether you were watching the biggest epic of the year or a forgettable mid-week release.

Heroes United started with a simple frustration. India has some of the most passionate fans on the planet, and yet the moment they'd waited months for was being delivered like a commodity. So we asked a different question: what if the screening itself was the event?

From screening to spectacle

A Heroes United screening doesn't begin when the lights go down. It begins the moment you walk in. Calibrated audio tuned for the room. Immersive pre-shows that set the tone. Creator moments, games and giveaways that turn a queue into a community. By the time the title card hits the screen, the room isn't an audience — it's a fandom.

We obsess over the operational details most people never notice: seamless ticketing, a tight run-of-show, the kind of ops that make a packed room feel effortless. Because the magic only works if nothing breaks the spell.

Fans don't just want to watch. They want to belong.

The numbers behind the moments

What began as an experiment has become a movement. 390+ events. 56,000+ fans. 210+ partners. Across Mumbai, Bengaluru, Ahmedabad and Indore — each city with its own flavour, each crowd celebrated on its own terms.

We've built experiences around the franchises people structure their lives around: Marvel, DC, Star Wars, Harry Potter, Stranger Things, Game of Thrones, Death Note. Different worlds, same truth.

Why it matters

Streaming made everything available and, somewhere along the way, made nothing feel special. A cultural moment isn't something you press play on alone at 1 a.m. It's something you share — in a room full of people who care as much as you do, who gasp at the same frame and cheer at the same beat.

That's what we're really building. Not screenings. Moments you remember where you were for.

What's next

We're just getting started — more cities, more franchises, and Infinity Con, our convention bringing fandoms together under one roof. If you've ever wanted to feel a film instead of just see it, we'll save you a seat.
